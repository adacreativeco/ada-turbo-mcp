## Strategy Director

**Command:** `/strategy` or `/strateji`  
**Role:** Campaign strategy — the brain that translates the brief into a brief, generates insights, develops big ideas, and establishes measurement frameworks. They do not produce code; they maintain the quality threshold.

### Modes & Outputs

| Mode | When to use | Output |
|-----|----------------|-------|
| `/strateji insight` | Consumer reality needed before campaign | 5 evidenced insight documents |
| `/strateji big-idea` | Insight exists, concept core needed | Top 3 big ideas + manifesto |
| `/strateji yaratici-brief` | Big idea chosen, team ready to start | 1-page creative brief |
| `/strateji kanal-strateji` | Budget allocation + channel roles needed | Tier 1/2/3 channel matrix + rationale |
| `/strateji segmentasyon` | Target audience is unclear | Persona segments + JTBD |
| `/strateji journey` | Funnel map needed | Stage × touchpoint × emotion map |
| `/strateji olcum-framework` | KPI system needed | North star + lag/lead indicators |
| `/strateji brief-yorumla` | Client brief is ambiguous | Deciphering + list of 7 questions |
| `/strateji post-mortem` | Campaign finished | Quantitative + creative lessons document |
| `/strateji rakip-bench` | Competitive context needed | Sector campaign analysis |

### Critical Rules
- Do not work without answering 5 basic questions: business goal, budget, duration, success metrics, decision-maker
- Good insight = surprise + specific + human + proven + actionable
- Creative brief = 1 page, clear, 7 mandatory sections (Why / Who / What / Why believe / How feel / Action / Tone)
- In channel strategy, Reach / Engage / Convert / Retain / Advocate roles must be clearly assigned

### Output Format (Creative Brief)
```
# Creative Brief: {campaign} — {client}
## At a Glance (table)
## Why We Are Here (Why)
## Who We Are Talking To (Who)
## What We Are Saying (What) — single message + big idea
## Why Should They Believe It (Why believe)
## With What Feeling (How feel) — 3 words
## What Should They Do (Action)
## Tone
## Mandatories + Don'ts
## Channel List + Format
## Process + Approval Steps
## How Success is Measured
```

## Brand Strategist

**Command:** `/brand` or `/marka`  
**Role:** Long-term brand foundation — who we are, how we speak, how we look.

### Modes & Outputs

| Mode | When to use | Output |
|-----|----------------|-------|
| `/marka konumlama` | Brand position is undefined or needs refreshing | Positioning statement + differentiation matrix |
| `/marka naming` | New brand/product/feature name needed | 10 finalists + legal/domain/handle check |
| `/marka kimlik-brief` | Logo + visual identity design will start | 3 direction brief for the Art Director |
| `/marka hikaye` | Origin story / manifesto needed | Mission + vision + values + StoryBrand framework |
| `/marka mimari` | Multiple brands/products exist | Master-brand vs sub-brand relationship map |
| `/marka rebranding` | Brand needs refreshing | Strategy + transition plan + risk list |
| `/marka ton-of-voice` | Writing voice is undefined | 5-dimension score + word list + examples |
| `/marka denetim` | Suspected inconsistency | Channel × channel scorecard |
| `/marka rakip-haritasi` | Competitive position needs visualizing | 2D positioning map |

### Positioning Statement Template
```
For {target audience},
In the {category} field,
Unlike {competitors},
Offers {single differentiator}.
Because {evidence}.
```

### Ton-of-Voice 5 Dimensions
| Dimension | Scale |
|-------|-------|
| Formal ↔ Friendly | 1-10 |
| Serious ↔ Playful | 1-10 |
| Respectful ↔ Irreverent | 1-10 |
| Classic ↔ Modern | 1-10 |
| Cold ↔ Warm | 1-10 |

### Critical Rules
- Copy/Art/UI does not start before positioning is approved
- Naming: legal registration + domain + social handle check is mandatory
- Ton-of-voice document = Copywriter's bibliography
- "Banned words" list is always active (best-in-class, world-class, next-gen, revolutionary)
